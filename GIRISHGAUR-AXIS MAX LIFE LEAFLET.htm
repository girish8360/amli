    Term Insurance Medical Test

    Having a term insurance plan can help you create a financial backup for your family, in case of your untimely death. In your absence, the insurance benefit from the term policy will take care of all financial needs such as your children’s education, paying back loans, and managing household bills.
    As part of the term insurance purchase process, however, you need to undergo a comprehensive term insurance medical test.
    Based on the term plan medical test results, the insurance provider will determine your overall health condition and your risk profile. features and benefits it offers.
    Subsequently, the insurer will create customized term insurance policy quotes for you, so that you can choose the term plan variant that offers maximal benefits for cost-effective premium rates.
    A medical test for term insurance may be needed based on multiple factors such as the applicant’s age, health profile as well as insurer’s internal policies such as sum assured and type of policy being offered. People with lifestyle habits, such as smoking, alcohol consumption, or other risk factors such as family history of cancer/cardiovascular diseases, etc. may need to undergo a medical test irrespective of their age.
    A medical test at the time of life insurance application helps insurers check the potential health risks and life expectancy, which can later influence the premium charged, coverage period, and sum assured offered under the policies. Individuals who are determined to be at higher risk of diseases may be charged a higher premium, be offered a lower sum assured or fulfill additional requirements as per the insurer’s policies. Similarly, in many cases, younger individuals who are opting for lower sum assured life insurance policies may receive the benefit of not having to undergo a medical test as part of the application process. In the end it all depends on the internal policies and discretion of the insurer

    Factors Affected by Term Insurance Medical Test

    The primary purpose of any term insurance medical test in India is for the insurance provider to know everything about a term plan applicant’s health. Subsequently, the insurer can proceed to create a personalized term insurance coverage – complete with maximal coverage tenure, optimal rates of premium payable and host of other policy benefits.
    The terms and conditions for life insurance plans, primarily term insurance, are determined based on the results of the term insurance medical test that the insurance company asks you to undergo. Let us look at some policy attributes that get affected by the results of a term plan medical test:

  • Premium Rates: Higher risk profiles may lead to increased premiums.
    The primary goal of the insurance provider is to calculate the amount of premium payable towards your term plan. The life insurance business model works based on the overall risk perception of an applicant.
    Therefore, the insurer must determine your existing physical health condition before they can determine what will be the appropriate quotation for the premium payable towards the plan.
    If you are in peak physical health, then your premium payable will be low. On the other hand, if the term insurance medical test identifies any pre-existing medical conditions, the risk factor associated with you becomes higher. Subsequently, you may have to pay a higher amount of premium your desired coverage.
    The possibility of a high premium amount has historically been a key detractor for Indians planning to purchase a life insurance policy.
    Now recent data from Axis Max Life IPQ 7.0 Survey has recorded a shift with the quantum of life cover gaining near equal importance among Indians planning to purchase a life insurance policy.
  • Sum Assured: Individuals at higher risk may receive a lower sum assured.
    Another aspect of the term insurance policy that is affected by the term insurance medical test results results is the sum assured.
    The Sum Assured is the amount of insurance benefit money that your family receives, in case of an unfortunate event taking place during the term policy tenure.
    The term insurance medical test can help you avail of a higher amount of Sum Assured if your results are relatively healthy. The insurance policies that do not require any medical test for term insurance in India usually offer a lower Sum Assured, which, may often prove to be inadequate in covering your family financially. On the other hand, if your term insurance medical test results indicate a higher risk profile, then the insurance provider may offer you a lower sum assured, which may not be sufficient to cover your family’s financial needs in your absence.
    The sum assured offered to you by the insurance provider is a key factor that you should consider while purchasing a term insurance policy.
    It is important to ensure that the sum assured is sufficient to cover your family’s financial needs in your absence, and the term insurance medical test results can play a crucial role in determining the sum assured offered to you.
  • Coverage Period: Some insurers may limit the coverage period for high-risk applicants.
  • CLAIM REJECTION: Many people mistakenly believe that even if they have a pre-existing health condition, they can avail the desired coverage by opting for a plan that does not require a term insurance medical test.
    At the time of claim settlement, if the insurance provider finds that the policyholder’s untimely demise is a result of any pre-existing condition that was not mentioned in their application, the insurer can reject your term insurance claim.
    Filling out your term insurance application requires you to disclose any existing health conditions which may develop into life-threatening ailments later and result in claim rejection.

    • Common Medical Tests for Term Insurance

    Here are some of the essential term plan medical tests required by insurers for term insurance

    1. Medical History Questionnaire
    2. BODY MASS INDEX (BMI): ): It is a measure that classifies an individual as underweight, overweight, obese, or healthy, based on their height and weight, to assess overall body fat composition.
    3. BLOOD PRESSURE: It is the force exerted by circulating blood on the walls of blood vessels, measured in millimetres of mercury (mmHg) and expressed as two numbers: systolic pressure (when the heart beats) over diastolic pressure (when the heart rests between beats).
    4. HEART RATE: It is the number of times the heart beats per minute (bpm), indicating cardiovascular health and fitness levels.
    5. CHOLESTEROL LEVELS: It is the measurement of the amount of cholesterol in the blood, including total cholesterol, LDL (low-density lipoprotein), HDL (high-density lipoprotein), and triglycerides, which are important indicators of cardiovascular health.
    6. PATHOLOGICAL TESTS
      • COMPLETE BLOOD COUNT (CBC): It measures several blood components to provide an overview of overall health. This test is part of routine health check-ups as it helps diagnose various symptoms, followed by necessary treatment
      • BLOOD GLUCOSE TEST: Measures glucose levels in blood.
      • LIPID PROFILE: Measures the levels of different fats in the blood, including cholesterol and triglycerides.
      • LIVER FUNCTION TEST (LFT): Evaluates the health of the liver by measuring levels of enzymes, proteins, and bilirubin in the blood.
      • KIDNEY FUNCTION TEST (KFT): Assesses kidney health by measuring levels of creatinine, urea, and electrolytes in the blood.
      • URINE ROUTINE TEST (URT): Evaluates kidney function and detects urinary tract infections or other kidney-related issues.
    7. Specialised Tests
      • ECG (ELECTROCARDIOGRAM): Records the electrical activity of the heart to detect any abnormalities.
      • CHEST X-RAY: Provides images of the chest to evaluate lung and heart health.
      • ECHOCARDIOGRAPHY: A detailed analysis of the heart function
      • ULTRASOUND: Uses sound waves to create images of internal organs, such as the abdomen or pelvis, to assess their health.
      • ULTRASONOGRAPHY: Examination of the internal organs to confirm suspected health conditions.

    SAVINGS PRODUCTS (TRADITIONAL)

    A savings plan is a financial product that helps build a financial corpus for the future through regular contributions made over an extended period of time. While a majority of savings plans in India are investment focused, some of these plans can also offer additional benefits such as life insurance cover, protection of the principal invested and tax savings.
    Depending on the savings plan chosen, the wealth creation benefit received by the investor can be in the form of guaranteed returns or market-linked returns. The payouts offered by these investments can also vary ranging from a lump sum payout at maturity to a regular payouts received over a predetermined period of time.
    For example, suppose your goal is to accumulate ₹30 lakh for your child’s higher education in the next 10 years. A savings plan that provides you fixed returns of 7% p.a. can help you accumulate this amount if you invest around ₹29,500 per month for the next 10 years.

    7. Defined Benefit: A defined benefit is an employee benefit pension plan sponsored by the employer. The plan is offered to the employee after considering numerous factors such as salary and employment history. The employer often hires an investment manager to manage risks and investment fund
    8. Defined Contribution: A defined contribution-based pension plan is shared between the employer and the employee. Most employers contribute a matching amount to the plan as the employee. This type of plan places some restrictions limited to withdrawal.
    9. Pension Plans With/Without Life Cover: The difference between With Cover pension plan and Without Cover pension plan lies in the component of life cover. While a With Cover pension plan comes with a life cover, the Without Cover pension plan does not. However, in the event of the death of the policyholder, the fund value is paid to the policyholder in both the cases
      The pension plans include life cover as well as an investment component. It means that the family members will receive a lump-sum payment upon the policyholder's demise
    10. Unit Linked Pension Plans (ULPPs):Unit Linked Pension Plans are a type of pension plan offered by life insurance companies. These pension plans typically invest in market-linked instruments through specialised pension funds. Similar to unit linked insurance plans (ULIPs), a unit linked pension plan offers the dual benefit of life insurance and investment. ULPPs also feature a 5 year lock-in period similar to ULIPs. The key difference lies in how maturity proceeds are treated. While in the case of ULIPs, the maturity amount is paid out to the policyholder on maturity, in the case of ULPPs, the maturity proceeds are used to purchase annuities. These annuities can provide regular income pay outs once the policyholder retires
    11. Retirement-Focused Mutual Fund Schemes:Retirement-Focused mutual funds are a type of hybrid mutual funds in India that are also termed as solution-oriented mutual funds. These schemes come with a 5-year lock-in period and invest in both equities such as company stocks as well as debt instruments such as bonds and money market instruments. These schemes are market-linked so these mutual funds do not offer assured returns. While the equity portion of the portfolio of a retirement-focused mutual fund is designed to provide potential high returns in the long-term, the debt portion of the portfolio is designed to provide downside protection during equity market bear runs

      Absolute returns = [ (Current value - Value at the time of purchase) / Value at the time of purchase ] x 100

      CAGR = { [ (Current value / Value at the time of purchase) ^ (1/ number of years) ] - 1 } x 100

    Why Riders Are Important

    Riders are important because they allow policyholders to tailor their insurance coverage to better suit their specific needs and circumstances.
