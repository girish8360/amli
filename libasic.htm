    - SIP investments happen at dfferent NAVs

    - Funds maintain cash holdings

    - Enter late in bull markets

    This section provides a concise comparison of how CAPITAL GAINS TAX (CGT) applies to different investment classes in India. It is based on the capital gains taxation framework introduced after the 2024 Union Budget (subject to future amendments)
    The primary message is that the tax you pay at the time of redemption or sale directly reduces your effective Return on Investment (ROI).

    Understanding Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

    .

    Capital Gains Tax (CGT)** framework in India after the changes introduced by the **Finance (No. 2) Act, 2024**, which continue to form the basis of the current regime. The Government simplified the holding periods and rationalised tax rates across many asset classes.
    Capital Gains Tax is the tax payable on the profit earned when you Sell an investment for more than its purchase price.
    Capital Gain = Selling Price − Purchase Price − Eligible Transfer Expenses
    CGT is payable only when the asset is sold or transferred, not while it continues to appreciate in value.

    Types of Capital Gains

    There are two categories:

  • STCG (Short-Term Capital Gain) -Asset sold before the prescribed holding perio
  • LTCG (Long-Term Capital Gain) - Asset sold after the prescribed holding period

    INTRO TO INSURANCE

    Why Insurance Is Essential Before Investments

    Life is uncertain. A sudden d eath, accident, or serious illness can completely disturb family finances and force people to break investments or take loans. That is why Insurance is not an investment—it is protection.
    Life insurance is a critical financial tool in India, playing a vital role in ensuring financial security and stability for individuals and their families. With India’s growing economy and increasing financial awareness, life insurance has become a fundamental part of financial planning.

    1. Financial Security for Family

    Life insurance acts as a safety net for families in the unfortunate event of the policyholder's death. It provides a lump sum payout (sum assured) to the beneficiaries, ensuring that their financial needs are met even in the absence of the breadwinner.

    2. Income Replacement

    For many families, the primary earner's income is essential for daily expenses, education, healthcare, and more.Life insurance helps replace this lost income, ensuring that the family can maintain its standard of living.

    3. Tax Benefits

    Life insurance policies offer tax benefits under Section 80C and Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, 1961. Premiums paid are deductible from taxable income, and the maturity proceeds are usually tax-free, making life insurance a tax-efficient investment.

    4. Long-Term Savings and Investment

    Many life insurance products, such as endowment plans, ULIPs (Unit-Linked Insurance Plans), and money-back plans, combine insurance with savings or investment.They help individuals accumulate wealth over time, which can be used for significant milestones like buying a home, funding education, or retirement

    5. Retirement Planning

    Life insurance policies like pension plans and annuity plans ensure a steady income post-retirement.These plans help individuals maintain financial independence during their golden years.

    6. Children’s Education and Future

    Specific child insurance plans secure funds for a child's education, marriage, or other significant expenses. They provide financial support even if the parent is no longer around.

    7. Protection Against Debt

    Life insurance helps prevent families from falling into financial hardship due to outstanding loans or debts left by the policyholder.Policies like term plans can cover liabilities such as home loans, personal loans, or business loans

    8. Affordable Premiums for High Coverage

    Term insurance plans offer substantial life coverage at relatively low premiums, making it accessible even for modest-income individuals.

    9. Support During Critical Illnesses

    Many life insurance policies come with critical illness riders or standalone critical illness coverage. These provide financial assistance to cover medical expenses in case of life-threatening diseases like cancer, heart attack, or kidney failure.

    10. Encouragement of Financial Discipline

    Life insurance policies require regular premium payments, promoting the habit of disciplined savings.This ensures that individuals allocate a portion of their income toward securing their future

    11. Economic Stability for the Nation

    Life insurance companies mobilize long-term savings, which are invested in infrastructure development and other economic activities.This contributes to the overall economic growth of the country.

    12. Customized Plans for Varied Needs

    The life insurance market in India offers a range of policies tailored to specific needs, such as term insurance, whole life insurance, ULIPs, endowment plans, and more. This variety ensures that individuals can choose plans based on their financial goals, risk appetite, and budget.

    13. Rural Penetration and Social Security

    Government-backed life insurance schemes like PMJJBY (Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana) have increased insurance penetration in rural areas. These schemes provide affordable insurance to low-income groups, contributing to social security.

    14. Peace of Mind

    Life insurance offers peace of mind, knowing that one's family is financially protected against unforeseen circumstances. It reduces stress about future uncertainties, allowing individuals to focus on other aspects of life.

    Conclusion: Life insurance is not just a policy; it is a commitment to securing the financial future of one’s loved ones. In a country like India, where joint family systems and financial interdependence are common, life insurance plays a crucial role in ensuring stability and continuity during challenging times. It is an essential tool for anyone looking to safeguard their family's financial well-being and achieve long-term financial goals.

    3. Supply constraints: fewer beds, skilled professionals, infrastructure costs, which push up costs.
    4. Out-of-pocket burden: Many treatments are paid by households directly, which makes inflation felt more immediately

    Conclusion: A realistic working range for medical inflation in India currently is about 10 %–15% per year, with perhaps 12-14% being a reasonable mid-point assumption.

    FEATURES MEDICLAIM HEALTH INSURANCE
    Purpose Mediclaim policies are designed to reimburse hospitalisation expenses, focusing on treating illnesses or injuries. Health insurance policies provide comprehensive coverage, including hospitalisation, pre-hospitalisation, post-hospitalisation, and sometimes even wellness and preventive care.
    Coverage Mediclaim policies typically cover hospitalisation expenses, including room rent, doctor's fees, and medical expenses. Health insurance policies offer broader coverage, including:
    1. Pre-hospitalization expenses (e.g., doctor consultations, diagnostic tests)
    2. Post-hospitalization expenses (e.g., follow-up treatments, medication)
    3. Daycare procedures (e.g., cataract surgery, dialysis)
    4. Alternative treatments (e.g., Ayurveda, Unani)
    5. Wellness and preventive care (e.g., health check-ups, vaccinations)
    Sum Insured Mediclaim policies usually have a lower sum insured, typically ranging from ₹50,000 to ₹5 lakhs Health insurance policies offer higher sum insured options, ranging from ₹2 lakhs to ₹1 crore or more.
    Premium Mediclaim policies generally have lower premiums due to the limited coverage. Health insurance policies have higher premiums, reflecting the broader coverage and higher sum insured.
    Tax Benefits Mediclaim policies are eligible for tax benefits under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act. Health insurance policies also offer tax benefits under Section 80D.
    Waiting Period Mediclaim policies usually have a shorter waiting period for pre-existing diseases (typically 1-2 years). Health insurance policies may have a longer waiting period for pre-existing diseases (typically 2-4 years).
    Renewability Mediclaim policies are usually renewable up to a certain age (e.g., 60-65 years). Health insurance policies are often renewable for a lifetime, with some insurers offering coverage up to 80-100 years.
    Portability Mediclaim policies may not offer portability, or the ability to transfer the policy to another insurer. Health insurance policies usually offer portability, allowing policyholders to switch insurers while retaining accrued benefits.
    DRAWBACKS OF MEDICLAIMS
    Limited Coverage
    1. Hospitalization Expenses Only: Mediclaim policies only cover hospitalisation expenses, leaving out other medical expenses like doctor consultations, diagnostic tests, and medication.
    2. No Coverage for Pre-Hospitalization Expenses: Mediclaim policies do not cover pre-hospitalization expenses, which can add up quickly.
    Low Sum Insured
    1. Inadequate Coverage: Mediclaim policies typically have a low sum insured, which may not be enough to cover all medical expenses.
    2. Insufficient Coverage for Critical Illnesses: Mediclaim policies may not provide sufficient coverage for critical illnesses, which can result in significant out-of-pocket expenses.
    No Coverage for Pre-Existing Diseases
    1. Waiting Period: Mediclaim policies usually have a waiting period for pre-existing diseases, during which no claims can be made.
    2. Exclusions: Some mediclaim policies may exclude certain pre-existing diseases or conditions altogether.
    No Coverage for Daycare Procedures Exclusions: Mediclaim policies may exclude daycare procedures, which can result in significant out-of-pocket expenses.
    Limited Renewability
    1. Age Limit: Mediclaim policies usually have an age limit for renewability, after which the policy cannot be renewed.
    2. Health Conditions: Some mediclaim policies may not be renewable if the policyholder develops certain health conditions.
    No Portability No Transfer of Benefits: Mediclaim policies may not offer portability, which means that policyholders cannot transfer their accrued benefits to another insurer.
    Limited Flexibility
    1. Inflexible Plans: Mediclaim policies often have inflexible plans that may not be tailored to an individual's specific needs.
    2. Limited Add-ons: Mediclaim policies may not offer additional coverage options or add-ons, which can limit their flexibility.
    Higher Premiums for Older Adults Age-Based Premiums: Mediclaim policies often have age-based premiums, which can result in Higher premiums for older adults.
    Limited Coverage for Modern Treatments Exclusions: Mediclaim policies may exclude modern treatments like robotic surgeries, cyberknife treatments, or gene therapies.
    Complexity Complex Policy Terms: Mediclaim policies can have complex policy terms and conditions, which can make it difficult for policyholders to understand what is covered and what is not.

    Overall, while mediclaim policies provide some level of financial protection against medical expenses, they have several drawbacks that can limit their effectiveness.
    In summary, while mediclaim policies provide basic hospitalization coverage, health insurance policies offer more comprehensive coverage, higher sum insured options, and additional benefits like wellness and preventive care.

      Absolute returns = [ (Current value - Value at the time of purchase) / Value at the time of purchase ] x 100
    2. COMPOUND ANNUAL GROWTH RATE (CAGR): Using CAGR, you can calculate the compounded annual growth rate that your investment has achieved over a specific time period. The formula is as follows:
      CAGR = { [ (Current value / Value at the time of purchase) ^ (1/ number of years) ] - 1 } x 100

    TYPES OF PENSION PLANS

    Understanding different types of pension plans available in the market is a critical step when you begin making retirement plans. It provides a closer look into how a particular type of pension scheme can benefit you, in particular. It also gives you the necessary tools to envision life after retirement.
    Your financial requirements are likely to transform in the future due to an array of reasons. Primarily, the loss of an income source is the most concerning thing about retirement for most people. Finding the right type of pension plan can ease your worries considerably.
    Here are some of the common types of pension plans in India:

    1. DEFERED ANNUITY: The deferred annuity type of pension plan lets the policyholders receive annuity on a later date through single or regular premium payments. Over the course of the policy, they can save a substantial amount of money to be received as a pension. You can also avail of tax benefits with this type of pension plan.
    2. IMMEDIATE ANNUITY: Under the immediate annuity type of pension, you deposit a lump sum amount and receive the annuities straightaway. It is up to you to choose from the range of annuity options and the amount to invest. In case of an unfortunate incident, the nominee is entitled to receive the benefits.
    3. Guaranteed Period Annuity: In this type of pension plan, the policyholder receives the annuity for a specific number of years. They can choose the period of payment at their convenience. The nominee of this type of pension plan receives the payments in case of the insured's demise.
    4. Life Annuity: This form of pension plan is active until the policyholder passes away, as the name implies. If their policy has a "with spouse" option, their spouse will be entitled to the pension payout if the insured passes away.
    5. Life Insurance in Pension Plans:Life insurance and investment are both included in such pension plans. It ensures that if the policyholder dies, the policyholder's family will receive a lump-sum payout. However, it is important to remember that the insurance payout sum of this form of pension plan might be lower than with a standalone insurance plan.
    6. Whole Life ULIPs:Standard pension and retirement plans cover you till the age of 70-80 years, depending on the insurance company. However, Whole Life ULIPs, as the name suggests, cover you for as long as you live (till 99 or 100 years). A Whole Life ULIP does not only provide death benefit, but maturity benefit as well.
    7. Defined Benefit: A defined benefit is an employee benefit pension plan sponsored by the employer. The plan is offered to the employee after considering numerous factors such as salary and employment history. The employer often hires an investment manager to manage risks and investment fund
    8. Defined Contribution: A defined contribution-based pension plan is shared between the employer and the employee. Most employers contribute a matching amount to the plan as the employee. This type of plan places some restrictions limited to withdrawal.
    9. Pension Plans With/Without Life Cover: The difference between With Cover pension plan and Without Cover pension plan lies in the component of life cover. While a With Cover pension plan comes with a life cover, the Without Cover pension plan does not. However, in the event of the death of the policyholder, the fund value is paid to the policyholder in both the cases
      The pension plans include life cover as well as an investment component. It means that the family members will receive a lump-sum payment upon the policyholder's demise
    10. Unit Linked Pension Plans (ULPPs):Unit Linked Pension Plans are a type of pension plan offered by life insurance companies. These pension plans typically invest in market-linked instruments through specialised pension funds. Similar to unit linked insurance plans (ULIPs), a unit linked pension plan offers the dual benefit of life insurance and investment. ULPPs also feature a 5 year lock-in period similar to ULIPs. The key difference lies in how maturity proceeds are treated. While in the case of ULIPs, the maturity amount is paid out to the policyholder on maturity, in the case of ULPPs, the maturity proceeds are used to purchase annuities. These annuities can provide regular income pay outs once the policyholder retires
    11. Retirement-Focused Mutual Fund Schemes:Retirement-Focused mutual funds are a type of hybrid mutual funds in India that are also termed as solution-oriented mutual funds. These schemes come with a 5-year lock-in period and invest in both equities such as company stocks as well as debt instruments such as bonds and money market instruments. These schemes are market-linked so these mutual funds do not offer assured returns. While the equity portion of the portfolio of a retirement-focused mutual fund is designed to provide potential high returns in the long-term, the debt portion of the portfolio is designed to provide downside protection during equity market bear runs

    Riders in life insurance are optional add-on benefits that can be attached to a Base policy by paying an additional premium. They help customise the policy according to specific risks such as critical illness, disability, accidental death, or premium-payment difficulties.
    Here are some of the Key riders available with Axis Max Life policies:

    Why Riders Are Important

    Riders are important because they allow policyholders to tailor their insurance coverage to better suit their Specific needs and circumstances. They provide additional financial protection against risks that may not be covered by the base policy, ensuring that the policyholder and their family are better prepared for unforeseen events. By adding riders, policyholders can enhance the value of their insurance plan and have peace of mind knowing that they have comprehensive coverage for a wider range of situations.
    A basic life insurance policy primarily covers death. However, many financial risks occur during your lifetime, such as:

  • Critical illnesses (e.g., cancer, heart attack, stroke, etc)
  • Disability (temporary or permanent)
  • Accidental death
  • Inability to pay premiums due to job loss or disability
  • Hospitalization expenses
  • Income loss due to death or disability
  • Long-term care needs
  • Other specific risks based on your lifestyle or family history

    HEALTHCARE INFLATION IMPACT

    Medical inflation is on the rise making treatments expensive. If you get hospitalized for a critical illness or lifestyle disease, you may end up losing all your savings. The only way to afford quality medical treatment during a health emergency is by buying a health insurance policy.
    Take a look at some of the top reasons to buy a health insurance plan below

    1. Beat Medical Inflation - A health insurance policy can help you pay your medical bills, including pre and post-hospitalization expenses, today as well as in future despite the rising medical costs
    2. Afford Quality Medical Treatment - It helps you to afford the best quality medical treatment and care so that you can focus only on getting cured.
    3. Fight Lifestyle Diseases - It allows you to pay for the long-term treatment of lifestyle diseases like cancer, heart ailments, etc., that have been on the rise with the changing lifestyles.
    4. Protect Your Savings - It helps you to protect your hard-earned savings by covering your medical expenses so that you can avail the required treatment without any financial worries
    5. Avail Cashless Hospitalization Facility - - It allows you to obtain a cashless hospitalization facility at any of the network hospitals of your insurance provider by raising a cashless claim.
    6. Get Tax Benefits - It enables you to save tax on the health insurance premium that you’ve paid under section 80D of the Income Tax Act for better financial planning.
    7. Ensure Peace of Mind -It allows you to obtain medical treatment with peace of mind as you do not have to worry about paying hefty hospital bills.

    Key Benefits of Health Insurance Plans
    in India

    Health insurance plans offer a variety of health benefits to the insured, depending on the plan.
    Following are the key benefits of buying a health insurance plan in India.

    1. Hospitalization Expenses: A health insurance plan covers the medical expenses incurred on getting admitted to a hospital for more than 24 hours (In Niva Bupa, it's only 2+ hours). It includes a room rent, doctor’s fee, medicine costs, diagnostic test fees, etc.
    2. Pre & Post Hospitalization Expenses: It covers the medical expenses that you may have incurred on an illness before getting hospitalised as well as follow-up treatment expenses incurred after getting discharged.
      The pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation expenses are covered up to a fixed number of days (60 days to 180 days usually)as specified in the policy document.
    3. ICU Charges: A health insurance plan also covers the cost of availing treatment in an ICU or Intensive Care Unit during Hospitalization
    4. Ambulance Cost: It covers the cost of ambulance services availed to reach the nearest hospital during a medical emergency
    5. Cashless Treatments: All health insurance providers in India offer cashless treatment facilities at their network hospitals. You do not have to worry about arranging money to pay the hospital bills if you get admitted to a network hospital, as it will be settled by your insurer under cashless claims.
    6. Maternity Benefit: Several health insurance companies offer maternity benefits, including delivery expenses, pre-natal & post-natal expenses and medical termination of pregnancy. They also cover the cost of treatment and vaccination of the newborn baby.
      However, all maternity insurance plans have a waiting period ranging from 3 months to 4 years.
    7. Day Care Procedures: It also covers the cost of availing day care treatments that require hospitalization of less than 24 hours
    8. Pre-existing Diseases: The right health insurance policy also provides coverage for pre-existing diseases after you have completed the waiting period. Usually, pre-existing diseases are covered after a waiting period of 2 to 3 years.
    9. AYUSH Treatment: It covers the cost of availing medical treatment through the AYUSH system of medicines that includes Ayurveda, Unani, Homeopathy, Siddha and Yoga
    10. Medical Check-ups: Most health insurance companies in India offer free preventive health check-up facilities to the insured at regular intervals depending on the policy terms and conditions.
